Snapping that goes further

Six ways to place a window, and an engine that handles the parts other apps ignore.

Drag-snap with a layout palette

Edges give halves, corners quarters. Drag to the top and a palette of layouts drops down — thirds, priority center, four columns, yours.

Snap assist

Placed one window in a layout? The remaining slots offer your other windows as one-click chips. Esc dismisses, or just keep working.

Layouts that adapt

Apps enforce minimum sizes — Xcode won't be a narrow column. RippleSnap learns each app's limits, warns before the drop, and re-balances the layout instead of overlapping.

Multi-monitor brains

Profiles remember each display setup separately. Unplug the monitor and layouts adapt; plug it back in and they restore automatically.

Rules, schedules, triggers

Slack always opens in the right third. Deep Work applies at 9:00 on weekdays. Steam opens and your Game profile follows.

Undo for your desktop

Every apply snapshots your windows first. One key rewinds; a timeline holds the last ten states. Off-screen windows get a rescue button.
